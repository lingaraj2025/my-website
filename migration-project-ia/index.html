📦 Case Study: Migration of content from Confluence to Document 360

**Project Focus:** Enterprise content migration, knowledge management, Document360, content governance.

1. Project Overview

The team needed to migrate scattered product documentation from Confluence into a centralized Document360 knowledge base. The goal was to improve content discoverability, reduce support dependency, and eliminate duplicate information.

  • Challenge: Technical and user-facing content lived across multiple Confluence spaces, making updates inconsistent and hard to maintain.
  • Objective: Build a single source of truth in Document360, with consistent structure, metadata, and version control.
  • My role: Content migration lead responsible for audit, content modeling, cleanup, and publishing workflows.

2. Migration Approach

Migration work included content auditing, taxonomy design, and content transformation to align with user journeys and technical workflows.

  1. Conducted a comprehensive content audit of Confluence pages, labels, and attachments.
  2. Designed a Document360 structure with clearly defined categories, tags, and search-friendly article titles.
  3. Standardized article templates, consistent headers, and cross-linking patterns to improve usability.

3. Implementation

To ensure a smooth transition, the migration was executed in phases and paired with rigorous review cycles.

  • Created a migration tracker to manage page ownership, status, and approval checkpoints.
  • Used Document360 import tools combined with manual cleanup for complex pages and embedded diagrams.
  • Validated migrated content with SMEs and product teams to preserve accuracy and relevance.

4. Impact

The migration created a cleaner, more searchable knowledge base and significantly improved how subject matter experts and customers access documentation.

  • Centralized content reduced duplication and improved governance.
  • Self-service engagement increased by approximately 85% through better search results and structured content.
  • Documentation updates became faster and more consistent across teams.
**Conclusion:** Migrating content from Confluence to Document360 created a scalable, user-focused documentation platform that supported faster onboarding and clearer internal collaboration.
